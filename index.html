Pressure ™ -- Real-Time Crisis Navigator

Real Time

Monitoring twitter allows Pressure to discover issues real-time by watching for activity spikes, no more waiting for the news or State Department to update their sites. Pressure gives you the alerts sometimes hours ahead of other information outlets.

Context Aware

Pressure knows your current location and the potential location of any activity and will give you phone numbers, directions and travel times to the nearest U.S. Embassy. It also gives you news and twitter feeds to keep you aprised of the current situation.

Easy to Use

Available on all types of devices, Pressure works to keep the information simple, relevant and in context. This allows the user to quickly and easily notify their emergency contacts and see where threats might be. Putting the right information at your fingertips.

Many organizations from the US and other countries have representatives working in Africa on humanitarian missions. These people are highly-trained and well-meaning, but they are often unfamiliar with the hazards from wars, terrorist events, and natural disasters that occur in many African countries. These travelers do not speak the multitude of languages that are used in Africa; they do not always know where to go for safety when an event such as those listed above has occurred; and they do not know who to turn to for assistance. Many national governments and aid organizations such as iSOS provide support for travelers, but they can still be caught unaware. There simply was no data available to assess the severity, the employees’ proximity to danger, or to evaluate the safest response to the incident. While the scope of this project would be limited to Africa, the tool can be used by any traveler in any country. We propose to provide a real-time solution to this problem. We will provide a mobile-optimized web app that can alert subscribers to danger, allow them to contact key people, and take the right action (shelter in place, escape) to be safe. This App will rely on the fact that Twitter is nearly ubiquitous in many countries in Africa. We monitor and analyze the stream of tweets from each part of the continent, and notify subscribers when the tweet stream indicates that an event of interest has occurred.

Workers can be taken by surprise when a dangerous situation arises. One of our team members has had employees working in Nigeria when a person came running in explaining that a bombing had just occurred in the town. If would have been invaluable for those employees to have relevant information at their fingertips that can alert them of the danger and help them avoid it is invaluable. This tool gives travellers/foreign aid workers the information they need to navigate a crisis situation.
